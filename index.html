What is blockchain?

How does Bitcoin gain value?

Bitcoin is a digital currency or cryptocurrency that runs on the blockchain. This means that no central authority such as banks or governments have control over it. Rather, it is decentralized and operates on software and computers around the world.

Blockchain is a new emerging technology that records information and data by storing it in blocks. These blocks of information are spread and duplicated as a digital ledger across a network of computers. If a hacker attempts to alter data, the other computers will recognize a discrepancy and eliminate it as the same transaction is duplicated on every computer.

Bitcoin gets its value from a variety of different factors including market demand, press coverange, supply, and availability. For example with positive press coverage, people will feel more confident and purchase Bitcoin which will increase its price and vice versa with negative press. These factors cause the price to become extremely volatile

Final Thoughts

Although President Bukele adopted Bitcoin as an official currency in efforts to boost aggregate demand and increase El Salvador's rGDP, the opposite will most likely occur. Maintaining Bitcoin is unsustainable and with the continued skeptism of cryptocurrencies, confidence among consumers and businesses will fall. Furthermore, the government investment in setting up Bitcoin payments will be lost as the supply of Bitcoin continues to decrease. In order to make back the lost budget, the government will have to make cuts which will cause the economy to fall into a recession, thus decreasing aggregate demand. Lastly, Bitcoin was designed to be decentralized and free of control by an authoritaitve figure. Bukele's plan to centralize and something meant to be decentralized essentially removes his control and power over his country's economy. For example if El Salvador were to enter a recession, it would be difficult for Bukele to introduce an expansionary policy to boost the spending in the economy. However, as El Salvador is the first country to officially adopt a cryptocurrency, it will be interesting to see how it plays out for the country.
